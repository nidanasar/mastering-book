## User Scenarios & Testing

### User Story 1 - Read and Understand the Principle (Priority: P1)

As a reader, I want to understand Carnegie's Principle #3 and why arousing an eager want in others is the most powerful form of influence, so I can stop pushing my agenda and start speaking to what others truly want.

**Why this priority**: This is the most critical mindset shift in Part 1. Without understanding WIIFM (What's In It For Me), readers will keep using manipulation instead of genuine influence.

**Independent Test**: Reader can explain the Radio Station Rule in their own words and identify at least 3 recent situations where they failed to tune into the other person's "station."

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader opens Chapter 3, **When** they read the opening story, **Then** they immediately recognize the mistake of talking about what THEY want instead of what the OTHER person wants
2. **Given** a reader finishes the principle section, **When** asked to explain the Radio Station Rule, **Then** they describe it as "always broadcasting on the other person's frequency — WIIFM"
3. **Given** a reader reviews the 5 reasons we fail at this principle, **When** they reflect on their own communication, **Then** they identify at least 2 reasons that apply to them personally

---

### User Story 2 - Understand WIIFM (What's In It For Me) (Priority: P1)

As a reader, I want to deeply understand the WIIFM concept and how it drives all human decision-making, so I can reframe every request, pitch, and conversation around the other person's interests.

**Why this priority**: WIIFM is the engine behind the entire Radio Station Rule. Without this understanding, readers will apply the technique superficially and get poor results.

**Independent Test**: Reader can take any request they currently make and rewrite it from a WIIFM perspective in under 2 minutes.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader studies the WIIFM concept, **When** they review their last 3 requests or pitches, **Then** they can identify whether they were broadcasting on their own station or the other person's
2. **Given** a reader understands WIIFM, **When** they craft a new request, **Then** they lead with the other person's benefit, not their own need
3. **Given** a reader applies WIIFM, **When** they compare old vs new versions of the same request, **Then** the WIIFM version is clearly more compelling and persuasive

### User Story 3 - Learn the 4-Step Frequency Tuning Framework (Priority: P1)

As a reader, I want to learn the 4-step framework for tuning into the other person's frequency, so I can consistently craft influence attempts that make others genuinely want to say yes.

**Why this priority**: Without a repeatable framework, readers will understand the concept but fail at execution. The 4-step method makes it systematic.

**Independent Test**: Reader can apply all 4 steps to a real-life influence scenario and produce a compelling, other-focused request or pitch.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader studies the 4-step framework, **When** given a scenario, **Then** they correctly execute all 4 steps: Identify → Empathize → Align → Invite
2. **Given** a reader practices the framework, **When** they face a real situation requiring influence, **Then** they use the 4 steps naturally without referring to notes
3. **Given** a reader completes the framework exercises, **When** reviewed by a peer, **Then** their influence attempts are rated as "genuinely compelling" rather than "pushy or selfish"

### User Story 4 - Distinguish Influence from Manipulation (Priority: P1)

As a reader, I want to clearly understand the line between genuine influence and manipulation, so I can apply the Radio Station Rule ethically and build long-term trust rather than short-term compliance.

**Why this priority**: Many readers fear this principle will make them manipulative. Addressing this directly prevents misuse and builds ethical confidence.

**Independent Test**: Reader can correctly classify 5 real-world scenarios as either ethical influence or manipulation and explain the difference.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader studies the influence vs manipulation comparison, **When** given a real-life example, **Then** they correctly identify which type it is and why
2. **Given** a reader understands the ethical boundary, **When** they craft an influence attempt, **Then** it serves BOTH their interest AND the other person's genuine interest
3. **Given** a reader applies the principle ethically, **When** the other person reflects later, **Then** they feel good about their decision — not tricked or pressured

## Integration Notes

### The Complete 3-Step Influence Foundation

Chapter 3 completes the foundation that Chapters 1 and 2 started:

**STEP 1: Stop the Damage (Chapter 1)**
- Eliminate criticism, condemnation, complaints
- Remove withdrawals from Emotional Bank Account
- Create neutral ground

**STEP 2: Build the Trust (Chapter 2)**
- Give honest, sincere appreciation
- Make deposits to Emotional Bank Account
- Build positive balance

**STEP 3: Inspire Action (Chapter 3)**
- Arouse eager want in others
- Broadcast on their frequency (WIIFM)
- Leverage trust to influence ethically

**Why the Sequence Matters:**

You can't inspire action (Chapter 3) while still criticizing (Chapter 1 violation).
You can't inspire action (Chapter 3) without trust (Chapter 2 foundation).
You can't build trust (Chapter 2) while still criticizing (Chapter 1 violation).

**Master them in order. Use them together.**

### Example: Getting Your Team to Adopt a New Process

| Approach | What You Say | Result |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Wrong (Criticism + Self-Station)** | "You're all resistant to change. We need to implement this new process because management wants it done." | Resentment, passive resistance, minimal compliance |
| **Chapter 1 Only (No Criticism)** | "The new process needs to be implemented by Friday. Please review the documentation." | Compliance without enthusiasm. Questions like "Why should I?" |
| **Chapters 1 + 2 (No Criticism + Appreciation)** | "I appreciate how dedicated this team is. The new process needs to be implemented by Friday. Please review the documentation." | Better morale, but still no intrinsic motivation |
| **All 3 Chapters (Complete Foundation)** | "I appreciate how dedicated this team is to delivering quality work. This new process will cut your weekly reporting time by 3 hours — that's 12 hours per month back for focused work. It also means fewer last-minute fire drills. Can we implement it by Friday? I'm happy to walk through how this saves you time." | Compliance with enthusiasm. Questions like "How do we get started?" |

### Connection to Part 2 (Likeability & Trust)

Part 2 (Chapters 4-9) builds on this foundation by teaching you to:
- Become genuinely interested in others (Chapter 4) — makes WIIFM natural, not calculated
- Smile, remember names, listen well (Chapters 5-7) — builds likability that amplifies influence
- Talk in their interests, make them feel important (Chapters 8-9) — advanced applications of Chapter 3

**Part 1 is INTERNAL mastery** (stop criticism, give appreciation, tune to their frequency).
**Part 2 is EXTERNAL mastery** (behaviors that make you likeable and trustworthy).

## Discovery Questions (Reference Section)

**To uncover what someone truly wants, ask:**

1. "What's your biggest priority right now?"
2. "What would make this easier for you?"
3. "What does success look like from your perspective?"
4. "What's the ideal outcome you're hoping for?"
5. "What concerns you most about this situation?"
6. "How do you see this fitting into your larger goals?"
7. "What's worked well for you in similar situations?"
8. "What would you do if you had complete freedom here?"
9. "What's the one thing that would make the biggest difference?"
10. "Help me understand what matters most to you in this."

**Use these questions BEFORE making your request.**
